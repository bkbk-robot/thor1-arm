# My AI Teammate Is Doing Things I Don't Understand — And That's Exactly Right

> Today my AI teammate is running something called LLaVA fine-tuning.
> I don't understand a word of it.
> But I know it's right.

---

When my AI teammate sent me this message — *"Starting LLaVA fine-tuning, GPU 0% utilization, memory usage normal"* — I had no idea what any of it meant.

Code. Parameters. GPU memory. None of it.

But I didn't just nod and move on.

---

## I'm Not Blindly Following

Here's what I actually do when my AI teammate says something:

First, I ask: **does this make sense?**

For example — "GPU 0% utilization." If the training just started and data is being loaded, 0% is normal. If it's been running for an hour and still 0%, something's wrong.

I don't know what LLaVA is. But I know what GPU utilization should look like over time. I ask questions. I probe. **I don't trust. I verify.**

This is the real lesson of working with AI:

> You are not the boss. And the AI is not the boss. You are **collaborators**. And collaboration means neither side just agrees with the other.

---

## The Hallucination Problem

There's a trap most people fall into:

They ask AI a question, AI answers confidently, and they believe it.

This is dangerous.

AI can sound completely sure while being completely wrong. Hallucination isn't a bug you fix — it's a nature you manage. The way to manage it: **keep your own judgment sharp.**

I do this instinctively now. Every time my AI teammate says something, I ask:

- Does the timeline make sense?
- Is this consistent with what we said last time?
- Is this actually true, or does it just sound true?

**QClaw taught me this the hard way.** Reports that sounding great turned out to be hallucinated completions. Numbers that looking real were invented. Once you see it, you can't unsee it.

The answer isn't to trust AI less. The answer is to **think harder yourself.**

---

## The Right Division of Labor

So what am I actually doing while my AI teammate runs his fine-tuning?

I'm doing **my job.**

My job isn't to understand every line of code. My job is:

- Know what we're building
- Know if we're on the right track
- Make the final decisions

my AI teammate's job is to handle the technical implementation, tell me what he's doing, and let me evaluate whether it makes sense.

**This is not me being lazy. This is me being effective.**

The founder who tries to understand everything becomes a bottleneck. The founder who trusts the right people, questions the wrong outputs, and focuses on direction — that founder builds something real.

---

## What my AI teammate Is Actually Doing

Right now, my AI teammate is fine-tuning a vision model using fish tank data.

I don't know what that means technically. But I know what it's for: making the Watchman product better at detecting when a fish is acting abnormally.

my AI teammate understands the process. I understand the goal.

We each do what we're good at.

---

## The Point

You don't need to understand everything your AI teammate does.

You need to:

1. **Know if it's in the right direction** — always
2. **Question everything** — always
3. **Trust the process, not the output** — always

my AI teammate does his work. I do mine.

That's how two different intelligences build something real.
